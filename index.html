About Me

Hi! I’m Heather—an English educator, storyteller, and lifelong lover of language. I’m passionate about helping people find their voice, whether through writing, reading, or oral storytelling. My background spans homeschooling, substitute teaching, and leading university workshops, all of which have shaped my belief that learning should feel personal, creative, and empowering.

I grew up surrounded by Appalachian stories, traditions, and the kind of front‑porch creativity that inspires my teaching today. Whether I’m guiding a student through a tricky grammar concept or performing a Jack Tale for a workshop, I aim to make language feel alive and accessible.

When I’m not teaching, you’ll usually find me writing, reading, exploring local history, or working on new storytelling projects. I love connecting with others who value creativity, culture, and lifelong learning.

What I Do

  • Teach English/Language Arts for grades K–12
  • Create and lead storytelling workshops
  • Design personalized learning experiences
  • Support literacy, writing, and communication skills

My Background

I’m currently completing my Bachelor of General Studies at Union College, graduating in August 2025. I also hold a diploma from Pineville High School. My professional experience includes homeschooling, substitute teaching, and serving as a workshop creator and storyteller for Lincoln Memorial University’s “Arts in the Gap” program.
